Platform Overview

This platform trains and assesses students' AI literacy skills for essay revision. Students have already written an essay under timed conditions and now use AI assistance to revise it.

Three-Mode Structure

1
Briefing Mode: Provides information about the exercise
2
Training Mode: Teaches students how to use AI effectively
3
Assessment Mode: Evaluates students' human-AI communication skills

Assessment Focus

Dual Assessment Approach

Essay Quality: Assessed using essay rubrics
Human-AI Interaction: Assessed using interaction rubrics

Key AI Skills - Rubric Focus

Essential skills for essay revision assessed in our rubric:

A
In-Depth Conversation with AI

Engaging in comprehensive, multi-level dialogue with AI assistants

B
Critical Review of AI Suggestions

Evaluating and justifying acceptance/rejection of AI recommendations

C
Refining Process

Iterative improvement through multiple revision cycles with AI feedback

Rubric for AI-Assisted Review

Criteria 1 (Limited) 2 (Basic) 3 (Developing) 4 (Proficient) 5 (Excellent)
A
In-Depth Conversation with AI
No exchanges or chat history provided; no conversation beyond initial input; no questions asked Sparse exchanges with incomplete or no chat history; basic conversation with one or two simple questions; lacks depth Adequate exchanges shown in chat history; moderate conversation with some relevant questions; shows some depth Robust exchanges with comprehensive chat history; in-depth conversation with detailed, relevant questions on all levels Extensive exchanges with thorough, well-documented chat history; highly in-depth conversation with insightful, multi-level questions
B
Critical Review of AI Suggestions
All AI suggestions accepted without evaluation; no critical thought Most AI suggestions accepted with little critical analysis Some AI suggestions evaluated; partial critical review with justification Most AI suggestions critically assessed; clear justification for choices All AI suggestions thoroughly evaluated; strong, evidence-based justification
C
Refining Process
No revisions made Minimal revisions with no iterative process Some revisions with limited iteration based on AI feedback Clear iterative process with multiple revisions based on AI input Extensive refinement with critical review of AI feedback at each step

Training Mode Overview

This training mode helps students learn four essential AI collaboration skills before their formal assessment. Students work with a sample essay and practice effective AI communication.

Student Experience Flow

1

Provide Contextual Information

Initial Interaction:

Chatbot: "Thanks for sharing the essay! Could you provide more info about the task you're working on and tell me what help you need?"

Student should share:

  • • Who they are (course, year level)
  • • What course they're taking
  • • The specific task/assignment
  • • Their goals for this session
Follow-up Question:

Chatbot: "How would you like to edit the essay?"

Student should mention:

  • • There are two rubrics for assessment
  • • They'll be evaluated on essay revision AND AI communication
  • • Their specific revision priorities

Key Learning: Students practice providing comprehensive context to AI for better assistance.

2

Negotiate with AI on Agenda and Goals

Process:
  • Students receive diagnosis of their essay against the rubrics
  • They must decide which issues to focus on
  • They should reflect on their strengths and weaknesses as writers
  • They need to consider time constraints

AI Guidance: The chatbot guides students to focus on macro-level issues first (content, organization) before micro-level issues (grammar, vocabulary).

Key Learning: Students learn strategic planning and priority-setting in revision.

3

Critically Review AI Responses

Expected Behavior:
  • Students should ask AI to explain suggestions with reference to rubrics and linguistic concepts
  • Students should question and evaluate AI recommendations
  • Students may seek a second opinion from a different AI (feature for future development)

Key Learning: Students develop critical thinking skills to evaluate AI assistance rather than blindly accepting suggestions.

4

Do Your Own Writing and Editing

Important Rule: AI should NOT do the editing for students - they must do the actual writing and editing themselves.

AI Role:

Provide suggestions and guidance only

Student Role:

Make final decisions and implement changes

Key Learning: Students maintain ownership of their work while using AI as a thinking partner.

Assessment Mode Overview

Assessment mode tests students' independent application of AI collaboration skills learned in training mode. This uses a minimal scaffolding approach with automated assessment of both AI interaction skills and essay improvement.

Core Assessment Philosophy

Minimal Scaffolding Approach

Unlike training mode, the assessment mode chatbot provides:

No guided prompts or step-by-step instructions

No scaffolding for skill application

Minimal system prompt focused on core behavior only

Student-driven interaction throughout the session

Independent Skill Application

Students must demonstrate mastery by:

Independently providing contextual information
Self-directing the revision process
Applying critical evaluation skills without prompts
Making strategic decisions autonomously

System Prompt Requirements

Minimal System Prompt Content

The chatbot system prompt should include only:

Core Behavior Reminder:

"You are an AI writing assistant. Provide suggestions and guidance to help students revise their essays."

Critical Constraint:

"IMPORTANT: Do not directly edit students' essays. Only provide suggestions - students must make all edits themselves."

Response Style:

"Provide helpful feedback and explanations when requested, but do not guide the conversation structure."

What NOT to Include

❌ No prompts to ask for context

❌ No guided conversation flow

❌ No reminders about rubrics or assessment criteria

❌ No scaffolding questions or suggestions

Student Expectations

Essay Submission Requirements

📝 Students provide their own original essay (not sample essays)

📄 Students must submit both original and revised versions

✅ Essays should be complete drafts ready for revision

Expected Skill Demonstration

Students should independently demonstrate:

1
Provide Context (Skill 1)
  • • Share course information, assignment details
  • • Mention assessment criteria and time constraints
  • • Set revision goals without prompting
2
Strategic Planning (Skill 2)
  • • Prioritize macro-level issues before micro-level
  • • Negotiate revision agenda with AI
  • • Make informed decisions about focus areas
3
Critical Evaluation (Skill 3)
  • • Ask for explanations of AI suggestions
  • • Reference rubrics and linguistic concepts
  • • Evaluate suggestions independently
4
Independent Editing (Skill 4)
  • • Perform all actual text editing themselves
  • • Make final decisions about revisions
  • • Maintain ownership of their work

Technical Background: API vs GUI

For students interested in technical aspects

What is an API?

An Application Programming Interface (API) is a different kind of interface compared to the Graphical User Interface (GUI) you normally see. While GUI has buttons, menus, and visual elements for human interaction, API provides a direct programmatic way for applications to communicate with each other.

GUI (Graphical Interface)

  • • Visual buttons and menus
  • • Designed for human interaction
  • • Point-and-click navigation
  • • Example: HKBU ITO website

API (Programming Interface)

  • • Direct data exchange
  • • Designed for applications
  • • Automated interactions
  • • Example: Our custom platform
